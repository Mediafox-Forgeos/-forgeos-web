### #1 — KYNEX

**Final Score: 95.0 / 100**

#### Etymology

From Ancient Greek **κυνεῖν** (*kynein*) — to drive, to urge, to set something into powerful forward motion. This root expressed not passive movement but directed, intentional propulsion — the force that moves something precisely toward its destination.

The suffix **-ex** (from Latin: out of, from within) creates a compound reading: *the driving force that emerges*. In the naming tradition of technology companies, -ex closes a name with authority: Apex, Codex, Index, Linux, SpaceX. KYNEX belongs in this lineage.

#### Pronunciation

- **English**: /ˈkaɪ.nɛks/ — *KAI-neks* (K as in kite + eye + neks)
- **Spanish**: /ˈki.nɛks/ — *KI-neks* (natural and clean in Spanish phonology)
- **Universal**: The K, Y, N, E, X sequence contains only phonemes present in Spanish, English, French, German, Portuguese, and Mandarin romanization.

#### Meaning

KYNEX carries the idea of a driving intelligence — not a passive platform, but an active force that propels networks, operators, and charging sessions forward with precision. It is the name of the engine behind the electrified world.

#### Brand Story

The global EV infrastructure challenge is not an energy problem. It is a coordination problem.

Millions of charging events happen every day across fragmented operators, incompatible protocols, and disconnected networks. Real-time decisions must be made across thousands of sessions simultaneously — which station, which tariff, which operator, which OCPP command. None of this happens by accident. It happens because something intelligent is driving it.

That something is KYNEX.

KYNEX is the AI operating layer that drives EV infrastructure forward: coordinating sessions, optimizing networks, managing operators, and making every electron count. The name comes from the Greek root for directed force — because this is not a product that passively observes. It drives.

#### Why It Works

- The **K opener** gives it weight and authority. Companies with K-openers (Kodak, Kia, KPMG, Kyocera) project substance and permanence.
- The **Y** in second position creates immediate visual distinctiveness. In a crowded landscape of vowel-heavy names, KY is rare, striking, and memorable.
- **NEX** — with its close resonance to "nexus" and "next" — implies connection and forward momentum without stating either.
- The **terminal X** closes the name with precision. It is crisp, decisive, and typographically powerful in all caps: **KYNEX**.
- **5 letters** hit the global sweet spot: short enough for instant recall, long enough to be distinctively owned.
- After hearing it once, it is retained. That is the hallway test, and KYNEX passes it.

#### Potential Slogans

- *"The intelligence that drives."*
- *"Drive. Connect. Scale."*
- *"Infrastructure that moves at the speed of intent."*
- *"Every charge. Orchestrated."*
- *"Powering the networks that power the world."*

#### Logo Direction

**Option A — Wordmark:** KYNEX in a geometric sans-serif (Söhne, Neue Haas Grotesk, or custom type). All-caps. Tight tracking. The Y functions as a subtle directional vector — the two upper strokes of the Y represent diverging paths; the stem represents the single intelligent layer that connects them.

**Option B — Symbol + Wordmark:** A minimal symbol derived from the K and the Y. The K's diagonal strokes and the Y's fork create a visual junction — the precise moment where direction is chosen. This is the charging network decision point made visible.

**Avoid:** Gradients, circular frames, lightning bolt motifs, or anything that visually references legacy EV iconography.

### #2 — VELOX

**Final Score: 89.0 / 100**

#### Etymology

Classical Latin **velox, velocis** — swift, rapid, fast. The direct root of the English word *velocity*. Used by Roman writers to describe the speed of rivers, horses, and messengers. It carries two millennia of linguistic authority.

#### Pronunciation

- **English**: /ˈviː.lɒks/ or /ˈvɛl.ɒks/ — both acceptable; VEH-loks is more common
- **Spanish**: /ˈbe.loks/ — **perfectly natural in Spanish**; identical to how a native speaker would read it

#### Meaning

Speed is only part of the story. VELOX is not just fast — it is *precisely fast*, like a Roman courier who arrives exactly when expected. For an EV infrastructure platform, speed means real-time session management, instant OCPP response, zero-lag billing. VELOX names that precision.

#### Brand Story

The electric vehicle revolution does not have a technology problem. It has a speed problem: the speed of provisioning new operators, the speed of resolving session disputes, the speed of billing reconciliation across currencies. Platforms built for yesterday's pace will not survive tomorrow's scale.

VELOX is the platform that moves at the speed the industry demands.

#### Why It Works

- The Latin root gives it **immediate global comprehension** without being descriptive. Anyone who has encountered the word "velocity" recognizes the semantic field without VELOX explaining itself.
- **V opener** gives it brand premium (Vercel, Visa, Verizon — V companies project forward motion).
- **X terminal** provides the same crisp closure as KYNEX.
- Perfect phonetics in both target languages (Spanish and English) — an exceptional bilingual advantage.
- The meaning story is elegant: *the platform for what comes next, at the speed it needs to arrive.*

#### Why It Didn't Win

- **Domain availability.** `velox.com` is occupied by legacy technology services. An AI SaaS company needs a domain that matches its ambition — `velox.ai` is available but requires explaining why .com was unavailable.
- **Originality gap.** VELOX is a real Latin word. Competitors, investors, and press will note this. The brand must be defended as a *repurposing*, not as a discovery.
- **Slight innovation discount.** The academic recognition of "velox" as a Latin adjective gives it heritage but slightly softens the *invented* quality that the most memorable tech companies carry.
- VELOX is a very strong brand. In a different competitive set, it wins. Against KYNEX, it loses by 6 points — specifically on originality, memorability, and the premium perception delta of a truly invented name versus a repurposed one.
